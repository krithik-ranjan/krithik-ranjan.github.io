Hello! I am Krithik Ranjan

I'm a senior at Cornell University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Design + Environmental Analysis. I am excited about creative learning experiences, assistive technologies, human-centered design and robotics. This page showcases some of the projects I have worked on.

PiDog

PiDog can follow your voice commands, be trained to recognize your hand gestures, and can also be taught new voice commands just like a real dog! This intelligent robot based on Raspberry Pi simulates the training of a pet with the help of speech recognition, computer vision, and machine learning.

Speculative Design Workbook

Series of speculative designs inspired by ideas from articles and research papers like Design Fiction by Julian Bleecker, Prefigurative Intervention by Andrew Boyd, and How Computer Systems Embody Values by Helen Nissenbaum. Compiled as a project for INFO 4240 Designing Tech for Social Impact.

Design Portfolio

Final portfolio for DEA 1110 Making a Difference by Design consisting of designs and prototypes of ideas on smart tattoos, healthy robotic environments, and environmental visualizations on an everyday object.

CroK64

An online, multiplayer version of the classic backyard game, Croquet, built using a FRDM-K64 microcontroller board as a gaming controller. Players move their board around like a mallet to hit their ball in different directions, trying to get it through all the wickets before the other player.

Test Board

An original integrated testing platform for the electrical sub-team of CU Autonomous Underwater Vehicle that connects to other boards in the electrical system and monitors the input, output and power signals to check the functionality and safety of the boards.
